01 / ATTRACTIONS

Must-see attractions
Banff National Park
BANFF & JASPER NATINAL PARKS
Of all Canada's top sights, Banff National Park justifiably ranks as many people's number one.
Stanley Park
VANCOUVER
One of North America’s largest urban green spaces, Stanley Park is revered for its dramatic forest-and-mountain oceanfront views.
Jasper National Park
JASPER TOWN & AROUND
Jasper is a rugged beauty; it's more raw and less tourist-pampering than its southern cousin Banff, and hence host to a more ambitious, adventurous visitor.
Lake Louise
LAKE LOUISE
Considered by many to be the crown jewel of Banff National Park, Lake Louise is nearly impossible to describe without resorting to clichés.
Moraine Lake
LAKE LOUISE
The spectacular, deep teal waters of Moraine Lake are one of Banff National Park’s most iconic sights.
Jasper National Park
BANFF & JASPER NATINAL PARKS
Encompassing 11,228 sq km of Rocky Mountains wilderness, including jagged peaks, vast forests, glacial lakes and the magnificent Columbia Icefield, Jasper shelters some of the most pristine natural features on the North American continent.
Distillery District
OLD TOWN, CORKTOWN
Centered on the 1832 Gooderham and Worts distillery - once the largest distillery in the British Empire - the 5-hectare Distillery District is one of Toronto's best downtown attractions.
Royal Ontario Museum
TORONTO
Opened in 1914, the multidisciplinary ROM is Canada's biggest natural-history museum and one of the largest museums in North America.
Niagara Falls
NIAGARA
Niagara Falls, Ontario, is a Canadian city at the famous waterfalls of the same name, linked with the U.S. by the Rainbow Bridge.
